The Brown Bear

About Brown Bears

"The Brown Bear (Ursus Arctos) is native to parts of Northern Eurasia and North America. Its status is currently least concern.

There are many sub species within the brown bear bear species, including the Atlas Bear and the Himalayan Brown Bear."

Species

  • Arctos
  • Collarus
  • Horribilis
  • Nelsoni (extinct)

Features

"Brown Bears are not always completely brown. Some can be redish or yellowish. They have very large, curved claws and huge paws. Male brown bears are often 30% larger than female brown bears. They can range from 5 feet to 9 feet from head head to toe."

Countries with Small Brown Bear Populations

Some countries with smaller Brown Bear populations include Armenia, Belarus, Bulgarian, China, Finland, France, Greece, India, Nepal, Poland, Romania, Slovenia, Turkmenistan, and Uzbekistan.
